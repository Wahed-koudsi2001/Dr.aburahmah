Types of Bariatric Surgeries

There are many approaches to weight loss, but the right procedure depends on individual health conditions and surrounding factors. For many, weight loss is a long journey and a major challenge, especially when traditional methods have failed to deliver lasting results.

Sleeve Gastrectomy

One of the most common weight-loss surgeries in the Gulf and Middle East. It’s relatively simple and safe: the stomach’s size is reduced to limit food intake, leading to significant and sustainable weight loss. It also helps improve health conditions related to obesity such as diabetes and hypertension.

Gastric Bypass

This procedure reduces the size of the stomach and bypasses part of the small intestine to limit calorie and nutrient absorption. It is particularly effective for those with severe obesity and conditions like diabetes.

Gastric Banding

A procedure in which an adjustable band is placed around the upper part of the stomach to limit its size and promote early satiety. Less common today compared to sleeve or bypass procedures.

Gastric Balloon

A non-surgical procedure where an inflatable balloon is placed in the stomach to occupy space and reduce food intake. Typically used as a temporary weight-loss solution.

Mini-Gastric Bypass (MGB)

A simplified version of the traditional bypass that combines stomach reduction with intestinal rerouting. It promotes faster weight loss and improves certain obesity-related conditions.

Biliopancreatic Diversion (BPD)

A more complex surgery involving stomach reduction and major intestinal rerouting to significantly reduce nutrient absorption. Recommended for patients requiring rapid and significant weight loss.
